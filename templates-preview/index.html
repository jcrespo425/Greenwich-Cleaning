Lead reply and quote follow-up templates.

Review a few tone options before choosing the standard reply language for Greenwich Cleaning. The goal is warm, credible communication that feels premium without sounding stiff or overproduced.

Best first reply Option A

Warm, polished, and broad enough to work for almost every new inquiry.

Best follow-up Gentle check-in

Feels professional and low-pressure after a quote has already been sent.

What we are optimizing for

Fast replies, calm professionalism, and language that feels polished without sounding corporate or stiff.

Use the first reply quickly

Send the initial reply as soon as practical after the lead comes in, even if the final quote still needs a little review.

Use the follow-up after the quote

Send the first follow-up only after the quote has already been shared and the lead has gone quiet for a bit.

Keep replies human

These work best when you personalize the town, service type, and one small detail from the inquiry instead of sending them untouched.

Stay low-pressure

The strongest tone for this brand is calm and useful, not urgent or sales-heavy.

Lead reply

First response templates for a new inquiry.

These are meant for the first reply after a form submission comes in and before the quote is finalized.

Option A: Warm and polished

Recommended Best for most leads
Subject Re: Your Greenwich Cleaning inquiry

Hi [First Name],

Thank you for reaching out to Greenwich Cleaning. I received your inquiry for [Service] in [Town], and I appreciate the details you included.

Based on what you shared, I would be happy to put together the next step and confirm any final details that may affect scheduling or pricing. If helpful, you can also reply here with anything else you would like me to know about the home, timing, or priorities.

I will follow up shortly with the next step.

Best,
Juan
Greenwich Cleaning

Tone: Warm, polished, and trustworthy.

Why it works: Feels personal, calm, and premium without sounding over-written.

Option B: Concise and premium

Shorter More formal
Subject Greenwich Cleaning inquiry received

Hi [First Name],

Thank you for your inquiry. I received your request for [Service] and will review the details shortly.

If there is anything else you would like included before I follow up, feel free to reply here.

Best,
Juan
Greenwich Cleaning

Tone: Efficient, crisp, and slightly more formal.

Why it works: Efficient and clean, but a little less warm.

Option C: Friendly and approachable

More conversational

I will take a look and follow up shortly with the next step. If there is anything you want to add in the meantime, just reply to this email.

Why it works: Friendly and easygoing, though slightly less elevated.

Quote follow-up

Follow-up templates after a quote has been sent.

These work well for leads that have gone quiet after receiving pricing or a proposed next step.

Option A: Gentle check-in

Recommended Low pressure
Subject Checking in on your quote

Hi [First Name],

I wanted to follow up on the quote I sent over and see if you had any questions.

If you would like to move forward, I would be happy to confirm timing and next steps. If now is not the right time, that is completely fine as well.

Best,
Juan
Greenwich Cleaning

Tone: Professional, calm, and non-pushy.

Why it works: Professional and easy to respond to without sounding salesy.

Scheduling-oriented

Best,
Juan
Greenwich Cleaning

Why it works: Good when you want the follow-up to feel practical and time-sensitive.

Suggested cadence

A simple rhythm for using these templates.

1. First reply

Send within the same business day whenever possible.

  • Use Lead Reply Option A most of the time
  • Personalize the town and service type before sending

2. Quote delivery

Reply with the quote separately once you have the actual numbers and timing.

  • Keep the quote clear and easy to scan
  • Invite questions instead of overexplaining

3. Follow-up

Use Quote Follow-up Option A if the lead has gone quiet after the quote.

  • Good around 2 to 4 days after the quote
  • Stay warm and low-pressure

4. Final close-out

Use Option C only when you want to politely clear the lead from your active follow-up list.

  • Best after a longer silence
  • Leaves the door open without chasing
